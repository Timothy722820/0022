Japanese festivals

Cherry Blossom Festival

Cherry Blossom Festival, also known as Sakura Festival, is a festival held in Japan from March 15th to April 15th every year. This is the season when cherry blossoms are in bloom from south to north in Japan. People hold sumptuous banquets under the cherry blossom trees, gather with their families, or invite friends to recite poems and paint while drinking and getting drunk.

The Kanda Matsuri

The Kanda Matsuri lasts about a week, but the busiest events take place the weekend before May 15th. Saturdays are mainly about parades, and Sundays are about mikoshi parades. The parade route extends from the vicinity of the shrine to Otemachi and Marunouchi in the center of Tokyo. The area around Nihonbashi is also an area where some mikoshi pass.

Seijinshiki

It is held on the second Monday of January. Local governments hold coming-of-age ceremonies and invite 20-year-old citizens to participate. it has now developed into a gorgeous and grand ceremonial gathering. In the new season of the New Year, all participants, regardless of gender, will dress up to attend, symbolizing farewell to minors and welcoming a new life.

Yamaga Lantern Festival

In this festival, women will work together to create a rare beauty. A thousand female dancers wearing paper gold lanterns dance gracefully to traditional tunes. The event is for women only, and costume rental is required to participate.

Bon Festival

Misato Town Oden Festival

Oden Festival expertise the life of a rice-planting girl and gain a deeper understanding of Japanese agriculture. During this festival, women plant rice rice in the shrine's sacred field. You need to rent costumes to participate in the festival.
